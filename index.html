English Proofreader and Copyeditor

As an author, you have spent months and maybe years gathering data and conducting your analysis to produce your paper/thesis/etc. but you’re afraid of getting rejected by the journal or of getting roasted at the examination panel due to poor language.

You’ve sent the paper to many friends whose English are supposedly good to have a look on the paper, and you’ve even sent it to native speakers of English so that they polish your language, yet they all fix just very minor errors and you still feel that’s way far from error-free language.

You still feel something is missing; you keep saying to yourself, “Am I stuck with such poor presentation of my work and my paper’s language can’t get fixed or improved?

What you don’t realize is that you don’t need native speakers of English and you don’t need to fire away as many copies as possible to as many friends as possible to help you improve your work’s language.

What you really need is someone who has STUDIED English grammar and has been TRAINED in spotting even the minutest of errors in your paper and has EXPERIENCE in editing which develops an eagle eye that doesn’t miss any error in the paper. Only then can you trust them to get an error-free text.

Assuming that a native English speaker is what you need to get an error-free paper is what’s keeping you from actually reaching that goal. Think about it for a second: let’s say you’re a native speaker of Arabic; would you be able to edit an Arabic-written text into error-free perfection in terms of language? Definitely NO! But a native speaker of Swahili who studied Arabic all his life and has been trained on editing Arabic will do a far better job! Don’t you think so?

services

Copyediting and proofreading

  • I will edit for and fix any errors in grammar, punctuation, spelling (US or UK, your choice).

  • I will check for redundancy and eliminate any monotonic or repetitive wording.

Paraphrasing + copyediting and proofreading

  • I will use my expertise in the language to produce the same message with different wording.
